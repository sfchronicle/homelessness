How big is SF’s homeless population?
San Francisco’s homeless population ranks below a handful of other major cities. Boston, Atlanta, New York City and Washington, D.C., all have more homeless people per-capita than San Francisco, according to Housing and Urban Development estimates. San Francisco, however, has the highest proportion of unsheltered homeless, counting 511 people on the streets for every 100,000 residents. (The size of the circles is proportional to the number of unsheltered homeless.) Of all major cities, Los Angeles has the second-highest unsheltered homeless population, at roughly 300 people per 100,000 residents.
